My name is Kris

I'm a 28 year old full-stack developer in Orange County :)

You'll fall in love with me at the first sight

I’m a full stack developer who’s passionate about technology and how it improves the efficiency of our lives.

I come from a family who loves to tinker and engineer, so I grew up with an interest in technology, but video games were my first true experience bonding with something electronic. Being an only child to a single mom, I spent a lot of time alone and found extreme joy in exploring the worlds of Azeroth, Halo, and Tamriel. However, being fascinated by technology, I had little to no experience coding or creating anything until later on in my life.

I went to college at Biola University, where I majored in Business Marketing/Management and had some exposure to Management Information Systems. This sparked my interest in systems and efficiency in the workplace. Post Biola I decided to move up to the Pacific Northwest to do some soul searching and moved all over Seattle, where I got a certificate in Holistic Landscape Design at Bastyr University. Shortly thereafter, I moved back home to California and was exposed to web development from some close friends of mine, where I was immediately enamored. I then enrolled in some classes on Codecademy.com - which led to me enrolling in an accelerated Full-Stack Web Development Program at LearningFuze.

While in the program, I spent time learning and developing in languages like JavaScript (ES5 + ES6), jQuery, React.js, HTML, CSS, MySQL and PHP. I also became extremely familiar with using tools like Git/Github/Version Control, npm, Postman, HTTPie, Slack, AWS, and EC2 instances. I spent time implementing all of these languages in solo projects as well as in a group setting in both a Hackathon format and in a Team project using the Agile Methodology. Experiencing this has given me a drive and a passion for something that I’ve never felt before and was the validation I needed to know I found the right field.

My experience in marketing and working under pressure gives me the ability to be an articulate communicator, and allows me to bring a unique perspective to the team environment.

I’m the marketing guy who’d rather be a dev.
